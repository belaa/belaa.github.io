Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument

DESI is an optical survey that seeks to study the effects of dark energy on the evolution of the universe. Using ten fiber-fed spectrographs, each processing 500 robotically-controlled fibers, DESI will be able to simultaneously image up to 5,000 galaxies and quasars. Over its 5-year lifetime, the DESI survey will obtain spectra of tens of millions of galaxies and quasars, and will generate the largest 3D map of the universe to date.

As a member of DESI, I work on developing software pipeline infrastructure for both simulation and validation purposes. Some of the projects I've worked on include simulating the eBOSS spectrograph and testing sky brightness models, as well as generating mocks to sample from for targeting using Gaussian Mixture Models and Extreme Deconvolution.

Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space and Time

LSST is an optical survey that will image over 35 billion objects to measure the expansion rate of the universe. Each field in its footprint will be imaged up to 800 times over a ten-year period through six different filters. I am a member of LSST's Dark Energy Science Collaboration, where I work on photometric redshift estimation using machine learning, with the goal of improving current, state-of-the-at methods to include the effects of blending. I've also worked on developing electro-optical testing software to characterize the performance of the LSST sensors during camera integration and testing.
