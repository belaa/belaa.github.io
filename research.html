Dark Energy Surveys

Large Synoptic Survey Telescope

LSST is an optical survey that will image over 35 billion objects. Each field in its 18,000 square degree footprint will be imaged up to 800 times over a ten-year period through six different filters. The telescope is currently under construction at Cerro Pachón Ridge in Chile.

Within the Dark Energy Science Collaboration (DESC) I work on photometric redshift estimation using machine learning, with the goal of improving current, state-of-the-at methods to include the effects of blending. I've also worked on developing electro-optical testing software to characterize the performance of the LSST sensors during camera integration and testing.

Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument

DESI is a spectroscopic survey that will create the largest ever 3D map of the universe using a fiber-fed spectrograph at the Mayall 4m telescope at Kitt Peak National Observatory.

As a member of DESI, I work on developing software pipeline infrastructure for both simulation and validation purposes. Some of the projects I've worked on include simulating the eBOSS spectrograph and testing sky brightness models, as well as generating mocks to sample from for targeting using Gaussian Mixture Models and Extreme Deconvolution.
