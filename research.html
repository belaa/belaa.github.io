Research

Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument

DESI is a ground-based dark energy experiment that will create the largest 3D map of the universe ever made using a fiber-fed spectrograph at the Mayall 4-Meter at Kitt Peak National Observatory.

I’m a part of the Data Systems working group in DESI, where I work on developing software pipeline infrastructure for both simulation and validation purposes. Some of the projects I’ve worked on include simulating the eBOSS spectrograph and testing sky brightness models using the Specsim package. I’ve also used Gaussian Mixture Models and Extreme Deconvolution to generate samples of galaxies and quasars to be used in the Targeting working group.

Large Synoptic Survey Telescope

LSST is an optical survey that will image over 35 billion objects. Each field in its 18,000 square degree footprint will be imaged up to 800 times over a ten-year period through a set of six filters. The telescope is currently under construction at Cerro Pachón Ridge in Chile.

I’ve worked on a variety of projects within the LSST Dark Energy Science Collaboration in both technical and analysis working groups. This includes improving the sky model used in the ImSim image simulation package, developing camera test software for verification and testing of the LSST camera As a recipient of the DOE SCGSR Award, I spent six months at SLAC Lab where I worked on verification and testing and instrument signature removal for the LSST camera. I’m currently working on photometric redshift estimation using a hierarchical bayesian model framework and self-organizing maps, with the goal of improving current state-of-the-art algorithms to include the effects of blending.
